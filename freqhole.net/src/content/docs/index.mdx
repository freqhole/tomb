## wtf is a freqhole?

a self-hosted music server and player: share your music library with yrself and yr frenz.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="self-hosted" icon="laptop">
    run on your own hardware (like a raspberry pi); D.I.Y. or D.I.E.
  </Card>
  <Card title="P2P streaming" icon="rocket">
    share with friends without port forwarding or dynamic DNS. read more about
    [shring with friends](/guides/sharing/).
  </Card>
  <Card title="works offline" icon="moon">
    caches music locally, keep listening without internet. sync yr tunez to yr
    mobile.
  </Card>
  <Card title="cross-platform" icon="puzzle">
    [desktop app](/getting-started/download/) for macOS and linux. CLI for linux
    geekz && headless servers. android app. web app has offline support & mobile
    responsive layout.
  </Card>
  <Card title="your files are read-only" icon="document">
    freqhole reads your music but doesn't write, move, rename, or delete your
    filez! everything is indexed in a sqlite database. read more about [music
    directories](/guides/scanning/).
  </Card>
  <Card title="music brainz" icon="magnifier">
    look up album info, track details, and cover art from MusicBrainz and Cover
    Art Archive.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## so watcha' want?

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="on your desktop or laptop" icon="laptop">
    use the **[desktop app (charnel)](/getting-started/)** — a GUI player and
    server management wizard, or the **[CLI](/getting-started/cli-setup/)** if
    you prefer the terminal or are running a headless server.
  </Card>
  <Card title="on your phone" icon="phone">
    use the **[web app](https://spume.freqhole.net)** in your mobile browser —
    it caches your library and music offline, so it keeps working when your
    connection drops. download & install the android .apk if that's yr thing.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>
